What we do
MOPS (Marketing Operations) handles end-to-end campaign deployment — from intake and build to QA and post-launch monitoring. We ensure campaigns are launched efficiently, accurately, and on time.
High-level responsibilities
  • Responsible for all campaign execution activities from intake to post-analysis.
  • Handles operational and tactical execution — audience segmentation, journey building, and launch.
  • Owns experimentation setup via Statsig, including setup, QA, maintenance, and reporting.
  • Manages day-to-day troubleshooting and monitoring of deployed campaigns.

MOPS Associate
▾
Advance review done to keep timelines on track. Key checks: creative assets, audience targeting, journey structure (Lucid Chart), experiment setup, campaign labels, deep links, XFN support, handlebar logic.

MOPS build & development — execution
MOPS Associate
▾
Full build once brief is approved. Stages can run in parallel:
  • MarTech/Enablement pod scoping (when Eng or MarTech support is needed)

  • Asset check — UTMs, snippets, handlebar logic, data feeds
  • Audience build in Iterable via segmentation tool or list import

MOPS campaign review & QA
MOPS Team
▾
Three-stage review process:
  • Self review — mandatory, done by campaign owner
  • Peer review — mandatory, clears campaign for launch

Deployment SOP
MOPS End-to-End Campaign Deployment Process
📅 Created: October 17, 2024 · 🔄 Updated: August 18, 2025 · ✍️ Marinelle Martos · 👥 MOPS Team
Provides a step-by-step guide on how MOPS handles and deploys campaign requests — from request submission to deployment. Ensures a clear and consistent approach for deploying campaigns efficiently.
Jump to phase
01 · Intake & assessment 02 · Build & development 03 · Proofing & QA 04 · Launch & post-launch ★ · Best practices
01
Intake & assessment
Steps 1–4 · Campaign requester → MOPS Team
1
Asana request submission
Campaign Requester
▾

Campaign requesters complete an Asana Campaign Intake Form. Two forms are available — select the one that fits your campaign's needs.

📎 Not sure which form to use? See the Intake Request Forms Guide ↗
✉️ Once submitted, requesters will receive an email confirmation with the details of their request.
2
Asana request received
Automated
▾

Submitted campaigns are automatically routed to the relevant Asana boards:

MOPS Requests & Projects ↗ — all campaigns requiring MOPS support
Brand Marketing Hotsheet ↗ — campaigns also requiring Brand support
Both boards are accessible to all stakeholders for real-time collaboration and progress tracking.
3
MOPS campaign assignment
Automated
▾

Assignments are based on three factors — in this order of priority:

Capacity — primary consideration; workload is distributed to avoid overloading any one member
Lane ownership — considered based on the actual needs of the campaign
Familiarity — prior experience with similar campaigns or stakeholders is a plus
The Team Lead has authority to override standard assignment guidelines when the campaign or situation requires it.
4
Campaign brief completion
Campaign Requester
▾
⚠️ MOPS will only officially engage with a request once the campaign brief has been completed and approved.
02
Build & development
Steps 5–6 · MOPS Associate · Stages can run in parallel
5
MOPS campaign acknowledgement
MOPS Associate · within 24–48 hrs of assignment
▾

MOPS strives to assign new campaign requests within 24 hours. Once assigned, the MOPS member has 24–48 hours to acknowledge the request by posting a Slack thread in the #marketing-ops-xfn ↗ channel.

💬 The Slack acknowledgement is how stakeholders know MOPS has picked up their request — don't skip this even if the launch is far out.
6
MOPS build & development — pre-build assessment
MOPS Associate — advance check before brief is approved
▾

Even before the brief is officially approved, MOPS can do an early review to flag gaps, align on timelines, and avoid last-minute surprises. Key items to assess before kicking off the Campaign Build ↗:

Creative handover

The bulk of MOPS work depends on creative asset delivery. Get visibility on timeline and complexity early. Campaign assets include: Email, Push Notifications, In-App Messaging, Landing Pages.

Audience criteria

Clarify who the target audience is and how they'll be pulled — Hightouch, Iterable segmentation, or list import. A detailed audience criteria is required for accurate segmentation — make sure you're aligned with the campaign owner.

Iterable segmentation — built directly in Iterable using the segmentation tool per brief criteria
Hightouch — data synced from the data warehouse into Iterable
List import (Iterable) — only Iterable-existing users should be imported to preserve a clean database and global holdouts. Share the list import query guide ↗ with the stakeholder and advise them to have it run by their DS team.
⚠️ For Iterable list imports, we only need users' email addresses — these map back to existing user profiles in Iterable.
List import (Pardot) — does not require the same Iterable query process
Journey structure

Confirm if a Lucid Chart is needed. If so: LM sends brief (with experimentation details and template) → MOPS builds chart → stakeholder approval → proceed to build.

Other items to check
Experiment setup — if Statsig is required, flag early
Campaign labels & attributions
Deep links / Branch links
XFN support needed: Eng, MarTech, DS
Custom events, profile fields, data catalogs
Tools or API integrations
Handlebar logic
7
MOPS build & development — execution
MOPS Associate · stages can run in parallel
▾

Once the brief is approved, the assigned Associate proceeds with the full build. All stages below can run in parallel depending on information availability.

MarTech ↔ Enablement pod scoping

Applies when Engineering or MarTech support is needed. The Associate collaborates with the Enablement Pod to define requirements, run data testing and validation, and complete an internal QA in Iterable before integrating elements into the campaign.

A MarTech request brief is typically completed by the campaign requester. The MOPS owner is briefed via Slack thread or Zoom walkthrough.
Creative handoff & dev work

Brand hands over assets → endorsed to Gooey (our vendor) for development. Launch dates are set or adjusted based on Brand's creative timeline.

Figma-Free process: For campaigns that don't need Gooey, MOPS repurposes existing templates — updating images and copy directly. Faster turnaround, no dev dependency.
Asset check

Once dev work is done, the Associate refines assets: renaming, adding UTM parameters, incorporating snippets, data feeds, and handlebar logic.

Email, Pardot Landing Page, In-App Message Snippets
Push notifications & Contentful landing pages — built directly by MOPS
Dynamic content & new tool requirements — MOPS tests handlebar logic and validates new functionalities; contacts MarTech for new custom events, data feeds, or API integrations
Audience build
Iterable segmentation — built using the segmentation tool per brief criteria
List import — users must already exist in Iterable; test and control groups added to the export sheet
Experimentation — Statsig used for test/control; groups encoded in export sheet for post-campaign analysis
Journey configuration

Set up the journey with the finalized audience or custom event. Apply delays, filters, holdouts, test splits, and experiments per the stakeholder's preferred cadence. Add campaign labels and UTMs for attribution. A Lucid Chart is strongly recommended.

Experiment setup (if applicable)
List-campaign label setup
MOPS Documentation | A/B Experiment Labels & Shipping Process
MOPS Documentation | Iterable Campaign Label
Statsig setup per the Marketing Statsig Experiment Setup Guide
⚡ Important: Build steps can occur in parallel — don't wait for one to finish before starting another if the information is already available.
03
Proofing & QA
Steps 7–9 · MOPS + Stakeholders · Can happen in parallel
7
MOPS internal proofing & QA
MOPS Associate
▾

MOPS conducts a thorough internal QA across all assets and platforms before sending to stakeholders.

QA of all assets — Email, Push, In-App, Landing Pages — across Iterable, Pardot, and Contentful
Rendering tested across devices (desktop, mobile, tablet) and email clients (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, AOL) in both light & dark mode
Deep links and Branch links verified as functional
SOPs and best practices followed for each touchpoint
QA checklist completed
8
Stakeholder proofing
Campaign requester + stakeholders
▾

An official proofing email is sent to all stakeholders in the campaign's proof distro. Stakeholders are asked to review and sign off on:

Visual sign-off — copy, layout, design, images
CTA links, deep links, and Branch links
UTM parameters
Handlebar logic
Dynamic content
💬 MOPS internal QA and stakeholder proofing can run in parallel — no need to wait for one to complete before starting the other.
9
Peer review & manager QA
MOPS Deployment team
▾

Final internal review covering journey configuration, experiment setup, and attribution (UTMs, campaign ID, export sheet).

Mandatory
Self review
Campaign owner ensures all aspects are ready before handing off.
Mandatory
Peer review
A Deployment Pod teammate reviews and gives clearance for launch.
Optional
Manager QA
Final check — should not block launch. Recommended for complex or high-impact campaigns.
Post-launch
24-hour post-launch check
Mandatory for triggered campaigns. Confirm journey is progressing as expected.

All comments and adjustments addressed
Workflow reviewed, finalized, scheduled, and enabled
MOPS Requirements section in brief updated for Data Science
Parent task in Asana marked as "Completed"

Post-launch monitoring

▾

Cross-check total sends against audience size
Set up Looker alerts (send volume, click rate)
Compile Lifecycle Marketing alerts document
Encode experiment lists in export Google Sheet
Monitor MOPS Metrics for QA score and issue resolution

Feedback survey
MOPS Associate — conditional
▾

Campaign is complex (POCs, launches, or large campaigns)
Stakeholder(s) are new to the MOPS team

Campaign intake best practices
For MOPS Associates — advance assessment guidelines
⏱
24–48 hour assignment window
Campaigns are assigned within 24–48 hours of receiving the request, after the Deployment Manager has reviewed and approved.
💬
Acknowledge early, regardless of launch date
Once assigned, Associates should review Asana tasks and the brief, then create a Slack thread to acknowledge the request — regardless of how far out the launch date is.
🔍
Advance assessment is critical
Helps with capacity forecasting, identifies pending assets and information needed for journey setup, and allows Associates to provide estimated timelines. Minimizes miscommunication and unnecessary back-and-forth.

Welcome to the Marketing Operations team at Thumbtack!
This playbook is designed to help you ramp quickly and succeed in your role. It's also your ongoing reference for how we execute campaigns, manage workflows, and collaborate with stakeholders.
What Marketing Operations does
The MOPS team is responsible for the end-to-end execution of marketing campaigns, ensuring initiatives are delivered accurately, on time, and aligned with business goals. We work closely with cross-functional partners across Lifecycle Marketing, Product Marketing, Brand, MarTech, Engineering, and Data Science to bring campaigns from request to launch.
The Deployment Pod focuses on campaign execution, quality assurance, and stakeholder coordination — while continuously improving processes and workflows. Ultimately, MOPS acts as the bridge between marketing strategy and technical execution, enabling scalable and effective campaign delivery.

🗺
End-to-end campaign deployment
Journey build & configuration, timeline management, and launch execution.
👥

Segmentation and validation via Iterable, Hightouch, or Pardot. List imports and audience QA.
✉️
Asset development & proofing
Emails, push notifications, in-app messages, and landing pages — built, proofed, and QA'd.
🧪
Experimentation
A/B test and Statsig experiment setup, QA, maintenance, and performance monitoring.
✅
Campaign quality & QA
Peer reviews, QA checks, and post-launch monitoring across all campaign types.
🤝
Cross-functional collaboration
Partnering with Lifecycle, Product, B2B, Brand, MarTech, Engineering, and Data Science.
A day in the life of a MOPS Associate
1
Review tasks in Asana — check assigned campaigns and deadlines, update subtasks and progress in the MOPS Requests & Projects Board, flag blockers early.
2
Monitor capacity & deadlines — review current workload and upcoming launches, identify risks to campaign timelines.
3
Execute campaign work — build journeys in Iterable/Pardot, landing pages in Contentful, experiments in Statsig, validate audiences, perform peer reviews and QA checks.
4
Stakeholder communication — clarify campaign requirements, provide progress updates, escalate blockers early.
5
Team alignment — participate in team syncs, support QA reviews, share updates in Slack.
