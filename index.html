About Marketing Operations (MOPS)

Who we are, what we do, and who we support across the organization. MOPS is the engine behind every campaign.

What We Do
Our core mission and responsibilities

MOPS (Marketing Operations) handles end-to-end campaign deployment — from intake and build to QA and post-launch monitoring. We ensure campaigns are launched efficiently, accurately, and on time.

High-level responsibilities
  • Responsible for all campaign execution activities from intake to post-analysis.
  • Handles operational and tactical execution — audience segmentation, journey building, and launch.
  • Owns experimentation setup via Statsig, including setup, QA, maintenance, and reporting.
  • Manages day-to-day troubleshooting and monitoring of deployed campaigns.
  • Partners with stakeholders to translate campaign strategy into technical execution.
Who We Support
Teams we frequently support and collaborate with
  • Lifecycle Marketing›
    MOPS builds and deploys all lifecycle email and push campaigns for this team — onboarding, retention, winback, and engagement journeys.
  • Product Marketing & Pro Experience›
    We support product launch campaigns, feature announcements, and in-app messaging for new and existing users.
  • B2B Marketing›
    MOPS handles outbound email campaigns, nurture sequences, and account-based marketing execution for the B2B team.
  • Partner Marketing›
    We support partner co-marketing campaigns, co-branded email sends, and channel partner communications on behalf of the Partner Marketing team.
  • Research›
    We assist with survey distribution campaigns and respondent outreach via email and SMS for UXR and market research initiatives.
  • Brand›
    MOPS executes brand awareness campaigns, newsletter sends, and broad audience communications on behalf of the Brand team.
  • MarTech›
    We collaborate closely with MarTech on platform integrations, data pipeline health, and tool provisioning for campaign execution.
  • Engineering›
    MOPS partners with Engineering on event tracking, API integrations, and technical troubleshooting for campaign infrastructure.
  • Data Science›
    We work with Data Science on audience segmentation logic, experiment design, and post-campaign performance analysis.
Tools & Platforms
Tech stack MOPS owned, co-managed, or only has build access
  • IterableOwned›
    Our primary email and push notification platform. MOPS fully owns this tool — we build, QA, and launch all campaigns directly in Iterable.
  • Salesforce Marketing Cloud (Pardot)Co-managed›
    Used for B2B email campaigns and lead nurture. MOPS co-manages this with the B2B Marketing and MarTech teams — we handle campaign builds while MarTech owns the instance.
  • HightouchBuild access only›
    Reverse ETL tool for syncing audience segments to campaign platforms. MOPS has build access to create and manage syncs but does not administer the platform.
  • StatsigBuild access only›
    Experimentation and feature flagging platform. MOPS sets up and QAs experiments in Statsig but does not own the platform — Data Science and Engineering are the admins.
  • ContentfulBuild access only›
    CMS used for in-app and web content. MOPS has build access to update campaign-related content entries but does not manage the Contentful instance.

01
The Challenge
Not a lack of quality — a lack of visibility and accessibility

MOPS has built something genuinely strong — detailed processes, well-documented standards, comprehensive onboarding frameworks, consistent QA practices, and a wide operational footprint. The challenge isn't that these things don't exist. It's that they're hard to find, hard to share, and hard for others to build on. As the team that sets the standard for how campaigns move through the organization, we have a responsibility to make that standard visible, accessible, and replicable.

Scattered sources of truth
We have a lot of documentation — it's just spread across Google Docs, Sheets, and a shared Drive that's difficult to navigate. Finding the right thing takes longer than it should.
Onboarding isn't interactive
We have an extensive, comprehensive onboarding framework — but it lives in static docs that require manual updates. New hires deserve an experience that's dynamic, structured, and self-serve.
Processes aren't easily discoverable
We have well-documented, consistent processes — but with so many of them, it's overwhelming to know where to start. The portal makes them organized, searchable, and easy to navigate.
Standards governance needs a home
MOPS sets the standard for campaign execution. For those standards to be replicable and sustainable, they need to live in one structured, maintained place — not scattered across individual files.
Stakeholders lack a starting point
Cross-functional partners work with MOPS regularly but don't always know the best way in — who to contact, how to submit a request, or what the team is capable of supporting.
Our work isn't fully visible
MOPS is often perceived as a campaign execution team. In reality, we manage a broad operational stack, support 9+ teams, run experiments, own QA standards, and enable the org's marketing output at scale.
02
The Opportunity
MOPS as Center of Excellence — and a model others can follow

This portal is more than an internal tool. It's a statement about how MOPS operates — and a blueprint that other marketing teams can learn from. When other teams hire their first Philippine-based new hires, or build their own ops functions, they can look at how MOPS does it and say: this is the standard.

Steward of standards & governance
MOPS becomes the org's reference point for how campaign operations should be run — not just for our team, but as a replicable, scalable model. From QA standards to onboarding structure, everything we do can be adopted by others.
Onboarding that scales itself
An interactive, always-updated onboarding experience — no manual upkeep required. And when other marketing teams bring on new PH hires without an onboarding process, this becomes their template: here's how MOPS does it, and you can too.
Empowered stakeholders, less friction
Lifecycle, B2B, Product, and other teams have one clear place to understand how MOPS works, who to contact, how to submit requests, and what to expect — reducing back-and-forth and misaligned asks.
Leadership visibility into MOPS' full scope
Directors and CMO-level stakeholders can see the real breadth of MOPS — not just campaign execution, but the standards, capacity, quality metrics, team structure, and strategic value we bring to the org.
03
The Solution
What the MOPS Team Portal is

The MOPS Team Portal is a self-contained, web-based internal hub that consolidates everything about Marketing Operations into one organized, navigable space. It is not a replacement for Asana, Google Docs, or Iterable — it is the front door to all of them, and to MOPS itself.

What it includes
  • Team directory with expertise, lane ownership, and Slack links
  • Always-on and recurring campaign reference with POC details
  • End-to-end campaign intake and deployment process documentation
  • MOPS standards, SLAs, and QA process
  • Live MOPS HQ Dashboard (embedded)
  • Office hours sign-up and stakeholder support channels
  • Library of templates, SOPs, and guides
  • New hire onboarding guide
Who it's for
  • MOPS team — primary users and maintainers; single source of truth for all internal processes
  • Lifecycle Marketing stakeholders — campaign reference, intake process, office hours
  • Cross-functional partners — B2B, Product, Brand, Engineering, Data Science
  • Leadership — Director of LM, CMO; team visibility, capacity, and quality metrics
  • New hires — structured onboarding reference, team facts, tool access guide

Award Overview & Guidelines
Program details, eligibility, evaluation process, and regulations

The QA Excellence Award program aims to recognize and reward outstanding performance within the team by incentivizing the highest monthly QA score achiever with a 1,500 PHP reward. This initiative promotes a culture of quality, accuracy, and continuous improvement.

1
Eligibility Criteria
  • Team members: All active Deployment Pod members involved in work evaluated through QA processes are eligible.
  • Assessment period: The program operates on a monthly cycle.
  • Performance metrics: The incentive is awarded based on QA accuracy metrics (must be 97% or above) and capacity handled during the month (used as a tiebreaker). Reference: Marketing Operations Metrics ↗
2
Evaluation Process
  • Data collection: QA scores are tracked and recorded throughout the month.
  • Score calculation: At the start of the following month, individual QA scores are averaged per participant. Each member's work is evaluated against predefined QA accuracy metrics, and scores reflect volume and effort via capacity points. See Capacity Guidelines ↗
  • Winner selection: The participant with the highest average QA score receives the 1,500 PHP incentive. Tiebreaker: higher total capacity points wins. If the tie persists, the incentive is split equally.
3
Reward Distribution
  • The 1,500 PHP incentive is provided through Grab reimbursement.
  • Winners submit the expense for reimbursement.
  • Rewards processed within five business days after the monthly evaluation.
4
Regulations & Guidelines
  • Fair evaluation: QA evaluations must adhere to standardized, objective criteria. Associates must have worked at least 12 business days and handled at least 4 capacity points to qualify.
  • Transparency: Monthly QA scores and winner announcements are shared openly. Participants may request detailed feedback on their scores.
  • Ethical conduct: Any attempt to manipulate QA scores will result in disqualification from the program.
  • Dispute resolution: Team members may raise concerns by submitting a formal inquiry to their manager. Resolutions provided within five business days.
5
Continuous Improvement
  • Feedback: Team members are encouraged to provide feedback on the incentive program for ongoing improvement.
  • Program review: The program is reviewed quarterly to assess effectiveness and ensure alignment with team goals.

Campaign Intake Process

How campaign requests enter the MOPS pipeline — from submission through to build assignment.

Showing only the steps where you (the requester) are involved. Switch to Full Process to see the complete MOPS workflow.
01
Intake & Assessment
How campaign requests enter the MOPS pipeline
1
Asana Request Submission
Requester
›

Campaign requesters complete an Asana Campaign Intake Form. Two forms are available depending on campaign type — select the one that best fits your needs.

Need guidance? See the Intake Process Guide ↗
2
Request Received & Routed
Automated
›

Once submitted, the request is automatically added to the MOPS Requests & Projects board ↗. If Brand support is needed, it also appears on the Brand Marketing Hotsheet ↗. Both boards are visible to all stakeholders for real-time tracking.

The requester receives an email confirmation once the submission is successful.

3
Campaign Assignment & Acknowledgement
MOPS
›

Campaigns are assigned based on team bandwidth, expertise, and lane ownership. The Manager retains authority to override assignments based on overall team priorities and campaign requirements.

Assignment target
Within 24 hours of submission
Acknowledgement window
24–48 hours after assignment
Acknowledgement channel
#marketing-ops-xfn ↗
4
Campaign Brief Completion
Requester Required to proceed
›
MOPS will not officially begin work until the campaign brief is fully completed by the requester. This is a hard requirement, not a guideline.

The brief is the foundation for everything that follows — audience targeting, asset creation, QA, and launch. An incomplete brief leads to delays, rework, and miscommunication.

5
MOPS Campaign Review & Assessment
MOPS
›

Even before the brief is fully completed, the assigned MOPS owner may do a quick preliminary review of the request to assess timelines and flag any immediate gaps. This is a proactive step — not a formal start of work.

Early assessment helps with capacity forecasting, identifying missing assets, and giving stakeholders realistic timelines upfront.
02
Build & Development
Campaign assets are built and prepared for review
6
Advance Assessment & Stakeholder Thread
MOPS
›

Regardless of launch date, the MOPS owner reviews the campaign brief and opens a Slack thread in #marketing-ops-xfn ↗ to acknowledge the request to stakeholders.

This advance assessment is a critical step — it surfaces pending assets, flags missing information for journey setup or audience targeting, and sets a realistic timeline expectation. It keeps stakeholders accountable and reduces unnecessary back-and-forth later.

7
Campaign Asset Build
MOPS
›

The MOPS owner builds all campaign assets — emails, push notifications, in-app messages, and landing pages — based on the approved brief. All assets are built to spec for both design and dynamic functionality.

03
Proofing & QA
Three-stage review process before any campaign goes live

MOPS Self-Review
Owner checks all assets

8
Stage 1 — MOPS Owner Self-Review
MOPS Owner
›

Before anything goes to stakeholders, the MOPS owner conducts a thorough self-review of all campaign assets — verifying design, copy, links, dynamic content, and full journey functionality.

This is the first line of defense. Every asset — emails, pushes, in-app, landing pages — must be confirmed working as intended, both visually and functionally, before moving forward.

9
Stage 2 — Stakeholder Proofing
MOPS + Requester Brand
›

Once the MOPS owner is satisfied with the build, proofs are sent to stakeholders via email. The proofing email includes:

  • A heads-up that proofs are on their way
  • A shared feedback document for consolidated comments
  • A clear feedback deadline based on campaign complexity

Once all feedback is received and updates are applied, the MOPS owner closes the email thread to signal completion. The purpose of this stage is Brand's creative sign-off and the Campaign Owner's content approval.

10
Stage 3 — MOPS Peer Review & QA
MOPS Team
›

The most thorough internal review stage. Three levels of review occur here:

Self Review
A final consolidation pass by the MOPS owner — incorporating all stakeholder feedback, ensuring everything is updated, and confirming readiness for peer and final review.
Required
Peer Review
An assigned peer reviewer — rotating each quarter — reviews the entire campaign using the detailed QA checklist. Covers all assets, functionality, tools, and journey/automation logic end-to-end.
Required
Final Review
Conducted by a Lane POC or Manager for complex or high-priority campaigns. This review is optional and should never block launch for standard campaigns.
Optional
04
Launch
Final checks and campaign goes live
11
Pre-Launch Final Checklist
MOPS
›

Before hitting send, the MOPS owner runs through a final pre-launch checklist — confirming all QA is complete, stakeholder approval is received, send time and time zone are correct, and the campaign is set to the right audience.

Best Practices
Habits that keep the pipeline running smoothly
  • Start early, even before the brief is done. A quick advance review helps surface blockers and gives stakeholders a realistic timeline from day one.
  • Always open a Slack thread to acknowledge the request. It closes the loop with stakeholders, creates a communication trail, and keeps everyone aligned.
  • Use the proofing email to set expectations, not just share proofs. Include the feedback deadline and what kind of input you need — creative vs. content.
  • Never skip the 24-hour post-launch check. A campaign launching is not the same as a campaign completing. Automation errors, journey exits, and delivery failures surface here.
  • Document as you go. Notes in Asana, thread updates in Slack, and closed email threads all reduce the cost of miscommunication and make handoffs seamless.

What Marketing Operations Does ›

MOPS is responsible for the end-to-end execution of marketing campaigns — ensuring initiatives are delivered accurately, on time, and aligned with business goals. We work closely with Lifecycle Marketing, Product Marketing, Brand, MarTech, Engineering, and Data Science to bring campaigns from request to launch.

Within MOPS, the Deployment Pod focuses on campaign execution, quality assurance, and stakeholder coordination — while continuously improving processes and workflows. MOPS acts as the bridge between marketing strategy and technical execution, enabling scalable and effective campaign delivery.
