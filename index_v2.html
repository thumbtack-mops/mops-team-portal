What we do
MOPS (Marketing Operations) handles end-to-end campaign deployment — from intake and build to QA and post-launch monitoring. We ensure campaigns are launched efficiently, accurately, and on time. Our scope covers Campaign Execution & Management and Experimentation.
High-level responsibilities
  • Responsible for all campaign execution activities from intake to post-analysis.
  • Handles operational and tactical execution, including audience segmentation, journey building, and launch.
  • Responsible for experimentation setup using Statsig — including setup, QA, maintenance, and reporting.
  • Owns day-to-day management and troubleshooting of deployed campaigns.

Campaign intake process
End-to-end steps for deploying a campaign — from request submission to post-launch review.
Intake & planning
1
Asana request submission
Requester
▾
Complete the Asana Campaign Intake Form — two forms available depending on campaign needs. Select the appropriate one before submitting.
◈ Action: campaign requester
2
Asana request received
Automatic
▾
Campaign is auto-added to the MOPS Requests & Projects board. If Brand support is needed, also added to the Brand Marketing Hotsheet board.
3
Review & task assignment
Deployment Lead
▾
MOPS Deployment Lead reviews the request and assigns it to an Associate based on bandwidth, expertise, and familiarity. Done within 24–48 hours.
4
Campaign brief completion & review
Requester + Approver
▾
MOPS only officially engages once the brief is complete and approved.
  • Lifecycle / Iterable campaigns → Josh Mack
  • PRO B2B & Sales campaigns → Brittany Slay
Build
5
MOPS review & assessment
MOPS Associate
▾
Advance review to keep timelines on track. Checks: creative assets, audience, journey structure, experiment setup, campaign labels, deep links, XFN support, handlebar logic.
6
MOPS campaign build
MOPS Associate
▾
Full build once brief is approved. Stages (can run in parallel):
  • MarTech/Enablement pod scoping
  • Creative handoff & dev work (Gooey or Figma-Free)
  • Asset check — UTMs, snippets, handlebar logic
  • Audience build in Iterable or list import
  • Journey configuration with delays, filters, holdouts
  • Experiment setup via Statsig (if applicable)
QA & proofing
7
Asset proofing
MOPS + Stakeholders
▾
Two types running in parallel: MOPS internal QA (rendering, deep links, QA checklist) and stakeholder proofing (visual sign-off, CTAs, UTMs, handlebar logic).
8
MOPS campaign review & QA
MOPS Team
▾
Three review stages: Self review (mandatory), Peer review (mandatory — clears for launch), Final review (optional for complex campaigns). Plus 24-hour post-launch review for triggered campaigns.
Launch & post-launch
9
Campaign launch
MOPS Associate
▾
Proofing approved, peer reviews finalized, workflow scheduled and enabled. MOPS Requirements updated in brief for Data Science. Asana task marked complete.
10
Post-launch QA monitoring
MOPS Associate
▾
Confirm users entered journey correctly. Cross-check sends vs. audience size. Set up Looker alerts. Encode experiment lists. Monitor MOPS Metrics for QA score.
11
Send MOPS feedback survey
MOPS Associate
▾
Send after complex campaigns (POCs, launches, large campaigns) or when the stakeholder is new to MOPS.
